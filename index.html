Types of Cyber Scams

Cyber scams are fraudulent schemes that trick people into giving away money, personal info, or access. Stay vigilant!

  • Phishing: Fake emails or sites pretending to be banks or companies to steal login details. Tip: Check URLs and never click suspicious links.
  • Ransomware: Malware that locks your files and demands payment. Tip: Backup data regularly and use antivirus software.
  • Identity Theft: Stealing personal info for fraud. Tip: Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Tech Support Scams: Fake calls claiming your computer is infected. Tip: Hang up and contact official support directly.

Cybersecurity Cases in India (2020)

In 2020, India saw a sharp rise in cyber incidents due to remote work and online activities during the COVID-19 pandemic. Here are some notable cases:

  • Phishing Attacks on Government and Health Sites: Hackers targeted the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) website and CoWIN vaccination portal with phishing emails and fake apps to steal user data. Impact: Exposed personal info of millions; led to stricter cybersecurity protocols. Lesson: Verify sources and use official apps.
  • Ransomware on Maharashtra Government: In February 2020, ransomware encrypted files on the Maharashtra Directorate of Vocational Education and Training (DVET) servers, demanding bitcoin. Impact: Disrupted education services; cost recovery efforts. Lesson: Regular backups and employee training on suspicious links.
  • Twitter Bitcoin Scam (Global but India-Affected): In July 2020, high-profile Twitter accounts (including Elon Musk and Barack Obama) were hacked to promote a bitcoin scam. Indian users lost crores in fake giveaways. Impact: Financial losses and eroded trust in social media. Lesson: Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) and avoid unsolicited crypto promises.

Overall, CERT-In reported over 1.1 million cyber incidents in 2020, up from previous years. For latest updates, visit CERT-In.
